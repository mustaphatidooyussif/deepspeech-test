1. The first permanent state in the territory of present-day Ghana dates back to the 11th century.
2. Ghana's population of approximately 30 million spans a variety of ethnic, 
    linguistic and religious groups.
3. Ghana is a unitary constitutional democracy led by a president who is both head of 
   state and head of the government.
4.  It became independent of the United Kingdom on 6 March 1957.
5. The flag of Ghana, consisting of the colours red, gold, green, and a black star, became 
    the new flag in 1957 when Gold Coast gained its name Ghana. 
6. Nkrumah was the first African head of state to promote the concept of Pan-Africanism. 
7. On 6 March 1957 at 12 am. Nkrumah declared Ghana's establishment and autonomy. 
8. On 1 July 1960, following the Ghanaian constitutional referendum, 1960 and Ghanaian 
  presidential election, 1960 Nkrumah declared Ghana as a republic as the first President of Ghana.
9.  the red represents the blood that was shed towards independence, the gold represents 
    the industrial minerals wealth of Ghana, the green symbolises the rich grasslands of 
    Ghana, and the black star is the symbol of the Ghanaian people and African emancipation.
10. Ghana is a member of the Non-Aligned Movement, the African Union, the Economic Community
    of West African States, and the Commonwealth of Nations.
